## Learning Outcomes

On completion of the course the student shall be able to:

- account for considerable in-depth knowledge on the most important battery systems and their role in the energy system and as consumer products,
- calculate the energy density and storage capabilities of batteries,
- analyse charge/discharge curves for the most common types of rechargeable batteries and interpret concepts describing battery performance, such as SOC and SOH,
- value and analyse computer simulations of energy storage alternatives from a system perspective,
- discuss the content in scientific reports,
- value pros and cons of different energy storage alternatives in relation to specific needs. [1](https://www.uu.se/en/study/syllabus?query=38010)

## Content

Description of primary and secondary batteries and their role in the energy system, including:

- different battery types,
- concepts describing the storage capabilities of batteries in the context of both energy and power,
- how an electric power system can affect the operation of a battery, for example in a vehicle or for large-scale storage,
- different modelling techniques from atomic to systems level,
- description of other storage systems such as water power, flywheels, supercapacitors, fuel cells, etc.,
- computer simulation of the performance of energy storage alternatives from a system perspective,
- pros and cons for different energy storage alternatives in relation to specific needs. [1](https://www.uu.se/en/study/syllabus?query=38010)
